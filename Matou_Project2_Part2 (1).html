    autopct='%1.0f%%', #Indicates the specified decimals you want; in this case, 0  
    startangle=90,         
    shadow=True,           
    counterclock=False)

plt.show()

  • The 15-24 sectionhad the most outliers even though it had the most concentrated data.

  • The 65+ group has no outliers but the lowest whisker (all the way at 0) compared to the other groups.

The next graph I did was a countplot to see the number of times each classifcation of literacy health showed across the sampled years, 2010-2018.

This visualization showed me that through the sampled years, there are many high counts of Excellent Literacy health, with 2018 having the most. The lowest was 2017, and then sky rocketing in 2018, causing me to question why there was such a jump in only a year.
